Nauset Manufacturing holds the exclusive rights to the first Modern O - Ring toilet coupler, wax-free toilet mounting system. A disruption to the wax and synthetic toilet flange industry.

Patent No. US12110673B2 Expires: 2036

The "Telescoping" Innovation

Traditional toilet flanges are made from an organic wax. The wax seal is prone to deforming and is almost guaranteed to leak at some point in the life of the fixture.

The Invention: A mounting assembly featuring an upper male flange that slides into a lower female flange. These two parts interface with a precision O-Ring (part 146) that creates a robust hermetic seal.

Claim 1 (The System): "A toilet mounting assembly comprising adjustable hollow bolts and clamping plates, a four bolt system. The sanitary connection is mechanically seperate from the toilet mounting bolts."

Claim 2 (The Seal): "A resilient, hydrophobic sealing ring disposed between the male and female flanges, maintaining a fluid-tight seal throughout the full range of telescopic adjustment."

Claim 3 (The Mechanics): "An upper male flange configured to slideably engage within the lower female receiver, permitting rotational alignment of the fixture bolts independent of the drain pipe orientation."

Patent No. US20250164056A1 Status: Pending

Sealing Flange Plug

A glued in flange plug for sealing an opening of a Johnny Joint and for being cut away by operation of a hole saw.

The Invention: A sealing plug allows for the Johnny Joint to be installed and protected until the toilet is ready to be installed.

Claim 1 (Protection): "When a DWV (Drain Waste Vent) pipe is installed, the Johnny Joint female flange is to be protected by this plug. Debris will be blocked from entering and hazordous waste or gasses are blocked from exiting"

Claim 2 (Glue In): "The standard Johnny joint flange is to be used with this plug. The plug is made of PVC, and such can be glued in place to create an air-tight seal."

Claim 3 (Drilling Out): "When it comes time to complete the installation of the sanitary system, the plug will be drilled out with a standard hole saw. This allows the male flange and toilet to be installed."
