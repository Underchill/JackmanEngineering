Nauset Manufacturing holds the exclusive rights to the first fully telescopic, wax-free toilet mounting system. A disruption to the $4B sanitary hardware market.

Patent No. US 12,110,673 B2 Expires: 2036

The "Telescoping" Innovation

Traditional toilet flanges are static. If the floor height changes during renovation (e.g., adding new tile), the flange becomes recessed, leading to leaks.

The Invention: A mounting assembly featuring an upper male flange that telescopes into a lower female flange. This allows the system to mechanically adapt to the floor height, eliminating the need for "stacking" wax ringsâ€”the #1 cause of toilet leaks.

Claim 1 (The System): "A toilet mounting assembly comprising adjustable hollow bolts and clamping plates, enabling secure engagement regardless of vertical pipe variance."

Claim 2 (The Seal): "A resilient, hydrophobic sealing ring disposed between the male and female flanges, maintaining a fluid-tight seal throughout the full range of telescopic adjustment."

Claim 3 (The Mechanics): "An upper male flange configured to slideably engage within the lower female receiver, permitting rotational alignment of the fixture bolts independent of the drain pipe orientation."
