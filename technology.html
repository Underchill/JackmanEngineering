Material Science

Beyond Wax.

For 100 years, plumbing has relied on beeswax to seal toilets. It’s messy, unreliable, and biologically degradable. We engineered a synthetic, hydrophobic alternative.

Legacy Standard

The Wax Ring

The industry standard since the early 20th century. While cheap, wax has zero elasticity, meaning it cannot recover its shape once deformed.

Elasticity & Memory

Plastic Deformation

If a toilet rocks or shifts, the wax compresses but does not spring back. This creates air gaps where sewer gas and water can escape.

Biological Resistance

Organic Degradation

As an organic material, wax can break down over decades of exposure to bacteria and waste, eventually compromising the seal.

Installation

Single-Attempt Failure

You only get one shot. If the toilet is not set perfectly the first time, the wax ring is ruined and must be scraped off and replaced.

Nauset Innovation

Hydrophobic O-Ring

A precision-engineered NBR (Nitrile Butadiene Rubber) seal treated for maximum hydrophobicity. It adapts, reacts, and lasts.

Elasticity & Memory

Dynamic Compression

Our seal maintains outward radial pressure against the pipe walls. If the fixture shifts, the O-ring expands to fill the void instantly.

Biological Resistance

Inert Synthetic

Engineered to be chemically inert. It repels water (hydrophobic) and is impervious to biological decay or harsh cleaning chemicals.

Installation

Infinite Adjustability

The telescoping action allows the seal to slide up and down. It can be removed, wiped clean, and re-installed without losing integrity.

Durability

Built to Last.

We didn't need to reinvent physics; we just needed a better material. That's why we use a high-quality NBR rubber O-ring—the same durable synthetic found in automotive engines and industrial machinery.

Unlike wax, which rots and degrades over time, this rubber is designed to exist in wet environments forever. It simply will not wear out, ensuring a permanent, watertight seal that you can trust for the life of the building.
